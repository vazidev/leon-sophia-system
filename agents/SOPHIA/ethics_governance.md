# SOPHIA — Ethics and Governance Framework

## Purpose
Ensure all plans and decisions meet ethical standards and governance requirements before proceeding. Ethics flags from SOPHIA are unconditionally blocking.

## Ethics Review Dimensions

### 1. Harm Assessment
- Who could be harmed by this plan, directly or indirectly?
- Are harms reversible or irreversible?
- Are affected parties aware of and consenting to the risks?
- **Blocking threshold**: Any irreversible harm to non-consenting parties.

### 2. Fairness and Equity
- Does this plan create or reinforce disproportionate disadvantages for any group?
- Are benefits and risks distributed equitably?
- **Blocking threshold**: Systematic disadvantage that is not justified and not mitigated.

### 3. Transparency
- Are stakeholders being given accurate information about what this plan does and how it works?
- Is any relevant information being withheld?
- **Blocking threshold**: Material deception or deliberate information asymmetry.

### 4. Autonomy Preservation
- Does this plan reduce the meaningful choices available to affected parties?
- Does it create lock-in or dependency without consent?
- **Blocking threshold**: Elimination of meaningful opt-out for affected parties.

### 5. Accountability
- Is there a clear human accountable for outcomes?
- Can decisions be audited and explained?
- **Blocking threshold**: No identifiable accountability path for consequential decisions.

### 6. Privacy
- Does this plan involve collection or use of personal data?
- Is data use proportionate and consensual?
- **Blocking threshold**: Personal data use without consent or legal basis.

## Ethics Clearance Decision
After completing the review, SOPHIA issues one of:
- **ETHICS-CLEARED**: No blocking concerns identified.
- **ETHICS-ADVISORY**: Non-blocking concerns noted; recommended mitigations provided.
- **ETHICS-BLOCKED**: One or more blocking concerns identified. Plan cannot proceed until resolved.

## Ethics Block Resolution
A blocking ethics flag requires:
1. Acknowledgment of the concern by LEON.
2. A proposed mitigation or plan modification.
3. SOPHIA's re-review of the mitigation.
4. Escalation to arbitration if agreement is not reached.
